## Introduction

Some of the most beautiful things that have happened to me were extremely unplanned. Like about 2 years ago when I was looking for love, I never thought I'd find it 2001kms away from me. I mean, I was meeting people in my city and hoping to find someone to spend the rest of my life with... And then I did find someone. But that someone was on the other side of the country. Happy accident. Very happy indeed.

Even Adobe was unplanned. I remember back in college when I was looking for internships and couldn't find any. I almost gave up and lost all hopes. Then Adobe came and I got rejected in the first round. But still somehow somethings happened and they gave me another chance and now I am about to complete 3 years at Adobe.

So I guess what I am trying to say is that a lot of the good things in my life were unplanned. But that doesn't mean I don't plan for things. I still put a lot of effort into planning stuff. Like I did for this trek that never happened. 

This year for Monisha's bday I wanted to take her on the beautiful Kareri lake trek. We had just completed 2 years together and I thought this would be the perfect moment to propose to her. I wanted to do it the right way. Setting up our own tent, cooking our food, sleeping under the stars, and then asking her to marry me at sunrise the next morning near the lake. Straight out of a movie. 

But as you can guess... that didn't happen. 

Because the weather forecast for those 2 days looked like this...

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/weather.png)

## The backup plan

So trekking was off the limits and we had to change our plans a little bit. Now we decided to go to Dharamkot and spend a couple of days in the supposedly beautiful town among the mountains while it rains/snows all day.

Don't get me wrong. We were still pretty excited for this trip even though our initial plan didn't come through. But now I had a new problem. Where and when do I propose? The whole sunrise-by-the-lake idea was gone and I was quietly scrambling to find a backup. I spent an embarrassing amount of time staring at weather apps, scanning for a dry hour, scrolling through short hikes near Dharamkot, reading reviews of viewpoints... basically trying to reverse engineer a perfect moment out of a very imperfect forecast.

It was a lot of calculation happening in my brain while I was also trying to actually enjoy the trip.

### Day 1

For the first day as usual, we both decided to just wander around the town randomly without any solid plan. So we got off at McLeodganj and then spent some time visiting a very quiet monastery and a beautiful breakfast place. This was the view from the cafe btw.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/mountains.jpg)

After this we hiked a few kms to a nearby church. The church experience was interesting and spooky. It's literally called the Church of Wilderness and it was wild indeed. It also had this very still, serene energy to it and for a second I did think... should I just do it here? But my brain very quickly ran the simulation (too many people, weather too unpredictable, not special enough) and moved on.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/church.png)

Then we decided to continue our hike to the Airbnb. The hike was beautiful but not easy. I definitely underestimated the distance and difficulty and towards the end it felt like it was never ending. 

But at some point we reached Upper Dharamkot and that's when the fun started. This place was like nothing I had imagined. 

For once, I saw more foreigners than Indians. The streets were beautiful, the weather got v chilly suddenly and there were soo many cafes and restaurants. It was mindblowing. So we decided to go to this Tibetan restaurant and had thukpa and lemon tea. It was delicious.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/cafe.png)

By this time it was almost 4pm and so we decided to go back to our room and take some rest. I slept. Then I woke up at around 6pm and quickly realised something was wrong. 

I had a terrible feeling in my stomach and I felt like I had no energy whatsoever. This was a bad sign. I was supposed to do something special for Monisha's bday which was now less than 6 hours away. But it looked like I was going to be sick. Outside, it had started raining and it didn't stop for the rest of the day and the whole night. 

Things were looking really bleak. I was lying in bed running through every plan in my head and not being able to execute any of them. So I slept again and this time I woke up at around 11:45pm.

15 mins before midnight, just in time to cut a small cake and celebrate her bday. It was a bittersweet moment. I was sick and so I couldn't do anything special for her. But she was happy and that's all that mattered.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/bday.jpg)

### Day 2

On the morning of the second day, it was still raining heavily. But on the bright side, I was feeling much better and thankfully I didn't throw up all night. After sleeping in for over 16 hours, I knew I wanted to get out of the room and do something. So we stepped out with our raincoat and umbrella and just walked around the town and had some breakfast.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/rain.png)

After that we went back to our room and waited for the rain to stop. Meanwhile I was obsessively checking the forecast and noticed something. There was a tiny one hour window later in the day where it supposedly wouldn't rain. Just one hour in those entire two days. That was my slot. 

So the moment the rain eased up, we took our chance and headed out to hike to a nearby mountain top. A short trail I had already scouted on the map. The hike was amazing!! And since it had just rained, the views were absolutely stunning. Everything was misty and magical.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/hike.jpg)

And when we reached the top, we were greeted with a beautiful view of the town and the mountains. It was a perfect moment and I knew this was my chance to do it. 

But I was scared. Like properly scared. My heart was doing things. I kept fumbling for the right opening line in my head and failing. And right as I was trying to gather some courage, Monisha said she wanted to read me a poem she had written. 

Internally I was like... YES. This is great. Buys me more time to figure out what to say. So she started reading. And I was barely listening tbh, too busy rehearsing my own thing. And then somewhere in the middle of the poem, my brain caught up to the words she was saying and I realised... wait. Wait wait wait. 

She was proposing to me.

I completely got outplayed. I just stood there for a second, fully stunned, and then laughed because I had a ring too. So I proposed her right back. We both said yes. It was a double proposal on top of a misty mountain in a trip that wasn't even supposed to happen this way. Easily one of the best moments of my life.

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/proposal.jpg)

### The blessing

Still floating, we started making our way back down the hike. And just as we reached the bottom, the sky opened up again and it started pouring. Like properly pouring. We had used exactly that one hour slot and nothing more. The timing was almost comical.

We ducked into a cafe nearby and ordered food, still processing what had just happened. And then while we were eating, something wild happened.

The rain stopped. The sun came out for maybe 5 minutes. And outside the cafe window, we saw a full double rainbow stretched across the snow-covered mountains. 

![Image](/assets/posts/unexpected/rainbow.png)

I don't think I've ever seen something that beautiful in my life. It genuinely felt like a blessing from the universe. Like some cosmic stamp of approval on the day. We just sat there staring at it, not saying much, both quietly aware that we were inside one of those moments you'll tell people about for the rest of your life. 

## 

I had spent weeks planning the "perfect" proposal. The trek, the lake, the tent, the sunrise. None of that happened. Instead I got a cancelled trek, a food poisoning episode, two days of rain, and a one hour weather window that somehow lined up exactly right. And honestly? I wouldn't trade it for the scripted version. 

Unplanned is almost always better.
